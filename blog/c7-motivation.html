Motivation

Wake Up. Kick Ass. Repeat.

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

When I left school let alone university I didn't know what I was most passionate about. Not until a few years after university by accident I started doing tech/coding work. Creating with code was a skill I found useful for myself and that opened up a lot of opportunities for me. I started teaching HTML/CSS to women who wanted to learn to code. I'ed finally found my higher purpose. I decided to start running workshops for people to give them the same opportunity and to train here at EDA to become more skilled. When I teach others I feel the 'flow' of doing work that is both engaging and pushes me. It's been a really long road of slogging away and trying to make ends meet to finally getting to the place I am now where I feel like I am doing rewarding work that helps other people. I definitely used other pleasure mediums to sustain my happiness weekend to weekend with "work" happening in the middle.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, what are your core values?

Happiness through appreciation. Confidence through hard work. Strength in numbers. Don't be a dick.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, what do you stand for?

Kindness. Loyalty. Understanding. Humour. Fun. Common sense.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

This was quite hard for me. There has been an overwhelming amount of learning we have been doing over the past weeks - lots of feelings of failure and struggle but not as much of success or that the success felt a bit dimmed by the amount of work we still have to do. It's not as easy to shake the feeling of a failure when you don't think you've resolved it yet.
