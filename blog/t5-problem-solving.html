Problem Solving

99 Problems and code is one... Hundred percent all of them.

What was the problem?

I've had so many problems but the two I most frequently suffer from are syntaxing issues and not understanding where to start.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I like to try console logging in the Chrome inspector or on a website like codepen.io. I also like to do the rubber ducky technique or the 'explain your problem to a five year old' technique to help simplify a problem. And psuedo code. Psuedocode the crap out of everything!

How did you feel throughout the process?

Because we're learning at such a fast rate the issues feel pretty never ending. By the time I get something it's already time to move to something else. I have brief moments of clarity and feel awesome for 2 seconds then go back to brain struggles.

What did you learn?

I like trouble shooting when I have someone to talk to - even if he is a rubber duck.
